Notes

  • Entries in parentheses are not x86 compatible. They are included either because they are closely related supplementary chips (e.g. 8087), influential predecessors (e.g. 8008), or part of a significant effort by a vendor to pivot away from the x86 architecture (e.g. Itanium).
  • Most release dates are those reported on Wikipedia articles. Not all of them are reliable, they are intended to provide an approximate timeline, but they were collected to the author's best capabilites. See the HTML source for comments on some resources.
  • Entries included are somewhat arbitrary, based on notable releases or features interesting to the author. Releases beyond x86-64 only focus on major architectural changes.
